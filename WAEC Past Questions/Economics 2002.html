ECONOMICS WASSCE 2002
Time: 1 hour
Number of Questions: 50

OBJECTIVE TEST

1. Economics is best described as the study of
A. the wealth of nations
B. how man consumes his products
C. how man provides for his everyday needs
D. the distribution of wealth

2. The pie-chart below represents the total population of a school 1200. The shaded area of 60° shows the population of the under-aged pupils. What is the population of the matured students?
A. 600
B. 800
C. 1000
D. 1200

3. The most frequently appearing value in a given data is the
A. mode
B. median
C. mean
D. range

4. Subsistence production means that goods and services are
A. produced for consumption only
B. sold to others
C. sold in a country
D. produced for the market

5. Warehouse facilities in the distribution and marketing of products are provided by
A. manufacturers
B. wholesalers
C. retailers
D. advertisers

6. Which of the following is a reward to a factor of production?
A. interest
B. donation
C. gifts
D. subsidy

7. Land is a factor of production because it
A. is a free gift of nature
B. contains valuable mineral resources
C. assists in the creation of utility
D. is owned by individual and governments

8. One of the advantages of large scale production is that
A. there is a rise in the cost of administration
B. consumers sacrifice their individual tastes
C. the firm can use labour-saving machinery
D. the demand for a firm's product becomes localized

9. Which of the following is an advantage of division of labour?
A. production of specialized goods
B. employment of experts
C. monotony of work
D. greater use of machinery

10. Despite the benefits that large firms enjoy, it could be limited by the
A. desire to make maximum profits
B. introduction of efficient transport system
C. growth of specialization
D. extent of the market

11. Localization of industry refers to the
A. concentration of the firms of an industry in a particular area
B. siting of industries in rural areas
C. even spread of industries
D. government policy of influencing the location of industries

12. Economists speak about opportunity cost when a customer
A. has the chance to minimize cost
B. has to forgo one thing in order to have another
C. can equate his fixed costs with his variable costs
D. is able to save part of his income

13. The cost which a firm would incur whether it is in production of not, is referred to as
A. average cost
B. variable cost
C. opportunity cost
D. fixed cost

14. Which of the curves in the diagram below represents the firm's Marginal Cost (MC)?
A. curve P
B. curve N
C. curve M
D. curve L

15. The location of timber and plywood industries in West Africa is mainly influenced by the availability of
A. transport
B. water
C. raw materials
D. labour supply

16. The main objective of setting up private businesses is to
A. protect the interest of the government
B. maximize profits
C. provide infrastructures
D. promote exports

17. Small firms are important for the development of a country because
A. the prices of their products are usually high
B. they render personalized services to the consumers
C. they usually provide goods for the elite
D. they do not normally provide after sales services

18. A limited liability company is owned by the
A. president of the country
B. workers
C. general manager
D. the shareholders

19. The National Electric Power Authority (NEPA) of Nigeria is a
A. public limited company
B. private limited company
C. private authority
D. public cooperation

20. When elasticity is zero, demand curve is
A. perfectly elastic
B. perfectly inelastic
C. concave
D. downward sloping

21. What type of price elasticity of demand is the diagram representing?
A. perfectly inelastic demand
B. perfectly elastic demand
C. fairly inelastic demand
D. unitary elasticity

22. An increase in the demand for butter reduces the demand for margarine; this type of demand is called?
A. competitive demand
B. elastic demand
C. derived demand
D. composite demand

23. A commodity is said to have derived demand when it
A. has a joint demand with another commodity
B. is demanded by the rich only
C. is demanded for immediate consumption
D. it is demanded because of what it can produce

24. Which of the following statements population is correct?
A. optimum population, whenever achieved does not change
B. under-population implies that available resources are not used to full capacity
C. over-population leads to higher per capita income
D. optimum population leads to overcrowding of cities

25. Malthus population theory stated that
A. high death rate may lead to low productivity
B. population may outgrow the means of subsistence
C. people will always decide to have children
D. migration may leave some of the parts of the world barren

26. One of the criticisms of population increase is that it results in
A. rise in people's standard of living
B. low level of illiteracy
C. low level of taxation
D. increase in government expenditure

27. Which of the following is not likely to be an effect of a growing population?
A. rise in demand
B. unemployment
C. fall in standard of living
D. high per capita income

28. Census figures of most countries in West Africa are inaccurate because of
A. low cost of enumeration
B. low per capita income
C. high degree of illiteracy
D. high level of civilization

29. Which of the following is a problem of trade by barter?
A. exchange rate determination
B. hoarding
C. how to produce
D. what to produce

30. Cheques are not money because
A. their use is confined to business hours
B. their are few banks in the rural areas
C. they are not generally acceptable as a medium of exchange
D. they are not issued by government

31. Which of the following is a liability of a commercial bank?
A. deposits
B. money at call
C. loans to customers
D. overdrafts

32. The value of money is best determined by its
A. size
B. purchasing power
C. portability
D. being recognized

33. Which of the following is a function of the Central Bank of Nigeria?
A. serving as custodian of important valuables
B. giving advice to customers
C. serving as bankers' bank
D. creating credit

34. Who among the following will benefit from inflation?
A. pensioners
B. savers
C. debtors
D. creditors

35. Inflation can be controlled by increasing
A. aggregate supply
B. government expenditure
C. aggregate demand
D. a deficit budget

36. Which of the following is a function merchant banks?
A. minting of coins
B. preparation of government budget
C. keeping watch on external reserves of the country
D. acting as acceptance houses

37. Personal savings are generally low in West Africa because of
A. the level of income of the people
B. the refusal of banks to grant loans
C. over-population
D. cheaper foreign currencies

38. Which business organization enjoys tax-free profits in West Africa?
A. cooperative societies
B. private limited liability companies
C. joint stock companies
D. partnerships

39. The greatest revenue earning industry in Nigeria is
A. construction
B. agriculture
C. manufacturing
D. mining

40. A market equilibrium exists when
A. no buyer goes home empty handed
B. demand and supply are increasing
C. demand and supply are equal
D. the price is fluctuating

41. The incidence of an in tax on a commodity with perfectly inelastic demand will be on the
A. wholesaler
B. retailer
C. government
D. consumer

42. Budget surplus implies that
A. expenditure equals revenue
B. expenditure is less than revenue
C. expenditure is greater than taxation
D. direct tax is more than indirect tax

43. International trade is based in the law of
A. absolute cost advantage
B. variable proportion
C. comparative cost advantage
D. mutual cooperation

44. Terms of trade is used to describe
A. quality of export
B. the direction of foreign trade
C. purchases on deferred payment basis
D. the rate at which exports exchange imports

45. A nation's net export is negative when her
A. stock of goods is declining
B. depreciation exceeds investment
C. export is adjusted upwards
D. imports exceed exports

46. Deficit in the balance of payments is financed through
A. capital account
B. current account
C. invisible trade
D. visible trade

47. Devaluation of currency is a country is likely to lead to
A. increasing population
B. increasing imports
C. exports becoming cheaper
D. reduced exports

48. Which of the following is not a benefit derived by Nigeria from the petroleum industry?
A. increased foreign exchange earnings
B. establishment of refineries and petrochemical industries
C. employment of a greater proportion of the population
D. development of airports, seaports and other infrastructures

49. Gross Domestic Product (GDP) at market prices plus net factor income from abroad gives
A. gross capital formation
B. net capital formation
C. disposable income
D. gross national product

50. An economic system in which the state owns and controls the means of production is known as
A. socialist economy
B. mixed economy
C. capitalist economy
D. welfare economy
